Overview:
This project explores employee sentiment at Samsung Electronics using Natural Language Processing (NLP) to analyze employee reviews. By applying sentiment analysis, topic modeling, and keyword extraction techniques, we extracted key insights into job satisfaction, workplace culture, and areas for improvement.

Approach & Methodology:

Data Collection: Processed structured and unstructured employee review data, including job roles, locations, ratings, and textual feedback.

NLP Techniques Used: 

Sentiment Analysis: Used TextBlob and VADER to classify reviews as positive, negative, or neutral.

Keyword Extraction: Applied TF-IDF to identify the most discussed topics in reviews.

Topic Modeling: Used Latent Dirichlet Allocation (LDA) to uncover key themes in employee feedback.

Data Visualization: Generated sentiment distribution graphs and word clouds for better insights.

Key Findings:

Positive Sentiment Dominance: Most employees appreciate salary, skill development, and workplace culture.

Areas of Concern: Career growth and job security had higher negative sentiment, indicating a need for improvement.

Work-Life Balance: While generally positive, some employees reported long working hours as a concern.

Department & Location Trends: Employee satisfaction varies across different departments and regions, with Mysuru and Maharashtra showing the highest positive sentiment.

Real-World Applications:

HR & Employee Retention: Helps Samsung Electronics improve workplace policies and retain talent.

Corporate Decision-Making: Data-driven insights for enhancing job satisfaction and productivity.

Competitive Analysis: Benchmarking Samsung’s work culture against industry standards.

Tech Stack: Python, NLTK, Scikit-learn, Matplotlib, Seaborn, Gensim

Skills: Natural Language Processing (NLP)
